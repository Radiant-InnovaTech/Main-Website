LAYER 02

Autonomous investigation orchestration

When a threat is detected, Nexus doesn't file a ticket. It opens a directed investigation, traverses entity relationships, evaluates competing hypotheses, and produces a full attack timeline — all before an analyst sees the first notification.

Graph traversalEntities, relationships, and signals are linked in a live knowledge graph updated in milliseconds.
Competing hypothesesMultiple threat narratives are evaluated in parallel and ranked by posterior probability before surfacing.
Full attack timelineA complete reconstruction of adversary actions from initial access to intended impact, ready in under 60 seconds.
Analyst in commandAll autonomous actions require human approval before execution. You review, approve, or override at every step.

Persistent operational memory

Every incident Nexus closes becomes institutional knowledge. Playbooks are written, updated, and retrieved automatically. New threats are matched against historical patterns before a single analyst types a query.

Incident retentionAll resolved incidents are persisted with full context, evidence chains, and analyst decisions.
Learned playbooksResponse playbooks are auto-written and refined from each resolved investigation — no manual curation required.
Instant recallSemantic search over all prior incidents gives analysts instant access to relevant precedent.
Tenant isolationMemory is cryptographically isolated per environment — MSSP customers share zero data across tenants.
